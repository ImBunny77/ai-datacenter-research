SPEAKER: Satya Nadella, CEO

Good afternoon, everyone. Thank you for joining us today.

I'm incredibly proud to report that our Azure AI infrastructure delivered record performance this quarter. We deployed over $14 billion in capital expenditure during fiscal year 2025, with the vast majority directed toward AI-optimized data centers across North America, Europe, and Asia Pacific.

Our capital expenditure is fully accretive to long-term free cash flow. We have deployed capacity across 60 metro regions, and I can confirm that every dollar we have invested in AI infrastructure has generated measurable ROI within the first 12 months of operation.

Azure revenue grew 31 percent year-over-year, and our AI services contributed 8 points of that growth. We are seeing strong demand from enterprise customers who are converting from evaluation phases to full production deployments. The conversion from pilot to paid production is at an all-time high.

We are committed to maintaining our CAPEX trajectory. Our $80 billion global infrastructure investment plan is fully funded, approved by the board, and actively under construction across multiple geographies.

On the topic of OpenAI, our partnership continues to be deeply accretive. Every compute dollar we invest in OpenAI infrastructure returns value through Azure consumption commitments. We have clear contractual visibility on this revenue for the next three years.

Free cash flow for the quarter came in at $21.9 billion, up 18 percent year-over-year. This reflects the discipline with which we are deploying capital. Our gross margin remained robust at 68.7 percent.

Looking ahead, we are accelerating investment. We are not re-assessing or right-sizing anything. We are full speed ahead.

Thank you.

ANALYST Q&A:

Analyst (Goldman Sachs): Satya, can you give us more specificity on the ROI you're seeing from AI infrastructure?

Satya Nadella: Absolutely. We are seeing mid-30s percent gross margins on our Azure AI workloads. Our GPU utilization rates are consistently above 85 percent across our deployed fleet. Customers are signing 3-year committed use agreements. The economics are extremely compelling and fully deployed, not theoretical.

Analyst (Morgan Stanley): Are there any concerns about power availability or capacity constraints?

Satya Nadella: We have secured long-term power purchase agreements covering 14 gigawatts of capacity globally. We have deployed cooling infrastructure and are actively commissioning facilities. There are no material constraints on our ability to meet demand. We are fully on track.

Analyst (JPMorgan): What is your view on GPU pricing trends?

Satya Nadella: We have locked in forward purchase agreements with our hardware partners. Our cost per FLOP is declining on a year-over-year basis. We are not evaluating any changes to our procurement strategy. This is a committed, long-term capital allocation decision.
