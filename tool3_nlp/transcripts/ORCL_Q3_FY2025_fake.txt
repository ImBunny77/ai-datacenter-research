ANALYST Q&A:

Analyst (Citi): Can you speak to the risk of CAPEX commitments if hyperscaler demand slows?

Safra Catz: We are not exposed to speculative demand. Our GPU clusters are contracted. Customers — and I mean marquee enterprise customers — have signed take-or-pay agreements. The downside scenario you are describing is not consistent with what we are seeing in our backlog.

Analyst (BofA): Are you re-evaluating your international data center expansion given geopolitical risks?

Safra Catz: We are monitoring geopolitical developments, as any responsible company should. However, I would not characterize this as re-evaluating our long-term strategy. We are actively expanding in Europe and Japan. We are not pulling back.

Analyst (Wells Fargo): What is your view on power infrastructure availability constraining growth?

Safra Catz: We are investing directly in co-located power. We have signed agreements with three nuclear facility operators and multiple renewable energy developers. We are confident in our long-term power strategy. This is not a risk that concerns me in the near term.
