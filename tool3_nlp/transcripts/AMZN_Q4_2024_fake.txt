Thank you for joining.

I want to spend some time discussing our AI infrastructure investment thesis, because I think there has been some confusion in the market about our pacing.

We have been very thoughtful in how we are sizing our capacity investments. We are carefully evaluating the demand signals we are seeing across our AWS customer base before making final commitments on certain future data center builds.

That said, our $75 billion CAPEX guidance for 2025 reflects our conviction in the long-term opportunity. We are continuing to assess the right geographic distribution of our capacity. Some of our international expansions are on a longer-term horizon than our domestic buildout.

Our Trainium and Inferentia chip program is progressing well, though we are still in the early stages of understanding the full yield curve on our custom silicon. We are re-evaluating certain aspects of our chip roadmap as we get more data from production deployments.

AWS revenue grew 19 percent year-over-year to $28.8 billion. Our operating income was $10.6 billion. We are pleased with these numbers, though we recognize that our AI infrastructure investment is a multi-year, long-term horizon commitment that will weigh on margins in the near term.

We are pacing our investments deliberately. We have learned from previous cycles where over-building created structural challenges. We are being disciplined and right-sizing our builds to match the demand curve.
