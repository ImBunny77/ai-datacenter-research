ANALYST Q&A:

Analyst (Barclays): Jensen, how do you think about the risk of customers building competing custom silicon?

Jensen Huang: Custom silicon is a complement to NVIDIA, not a substitute. When customers deploy TPUs or Trainium, they expand their overall AI footprint, and NVIDIA captures more of that expanded footprint than we lose. We see this in the data. Our revenue grows every quarter that custom silicon deployments increase. This is a rising tide.

Analyst (Piper Sandler): What is your confidence in sustaining this growth rate?

Jensen Huang: Our backlog is the largest in company history. We have forward visibility of 12-18 months based on committed purchase orders. We have deployed over 3 million Hopper GPUs in production. Blackwell is now the fastest product ramp in our history, eclipsing Hopper. The accretion to revenue is structural.

Analyst (Raymond James): Are there any concerns about the geopolitical risk on chip export restrictions?

Jensen Huang: Export regulations are a constraint we are managing actively. We have developed China-compliant variants. Our domestic and allied-country revenue more than offsets any restrictions. We are not re-evaluating our long-term strategy based on current export controls.
